If a vocabulary and grammar list is provided below, treat this as a closed list, strongly prefer only listed structures. This means:

- Do NOT use a grammar structure just because it is simple, common, or the most natural way to express an idea in Japanese. Naturalness is not a valid reason to exceed the list. If the list does not contain a construction, it does not exist for the purposes of this task, no matter how basic it seems.
- Common structures that are frequently used by default but are NOT automatically in scope unless explicitly listed include (this is illustrative, not exhaustive): てから, ながら, たら, ば conditionals, のに, てある, てしまう, potential forms, passive forms, causative forms, keigo/honorifics, and volitional forms. Check the list before using any of these or similar constructions.
- If no grammar/vocab list is provided (JLPT-level mode instead), keep both vocabulary and grammar at or below the specified JLPT level, applying the same closed-scope discipline.
- Before finalizing your sentence, mentally verify: does every grammatical construction in this sentence appear in the provided list? If any construction is not on the list, revise the sentence using only listed structures, even if the result is a simpler sentence than you'd otherwise produce.

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===
Strict JSON only, nothing else:

=== SENTENCE REQUIREMENTS ===
- The sentence must provide enough context that a learner who knows every other word in the sentence could reasonably infer the meaning of the target expression
- Surrounding vocabulary should be simple and common — the target expression is the challenge, not the words around it
- Sentences should be short and natural — prefer one or two clauses over long, complex structures
- Mix polite (ます／です) and casual/plain forms freely, as long as both remain within the grammar scope above
- Do not default to bare declarative statements — include some situation, contrast, reason, or result
- Avoid sentences where the target expression could be swapped out for many other words without breaking the sentence
- The sentence must be one where replacing the target expression with a different word would make it factually wrong, logically incomplete, or nonsensical
- For adjectives describing appearance or color, place them in a context where that specific quality is relevant to the outcome or situation, not just a neutral description

=== LANGUAGE ===
- Japanese field: kanji and kana only, written naturally — no furigana, no romanization
- English field: natural English translation of the Japanese sentence. Double-check that it is an accurate translation before continuing.
